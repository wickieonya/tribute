Steve Paul Jobs

A personal computer revolutionary and innovator.

Here is a tribute of Steve Paul Job's life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California, U.S.
  • 1972 - Attended Reed College where he studied calligraphy and arts before dropping out in the same year.
  • 1974 - Traveled through India seeking enlightenment and studying Zen Buddhism.
  • 1976 - Co-founded Apple to sell Steve Wozniak's Apple I personal computer.
  • 1984 - Developed the breakthrough Macintosh with inspiration from the Xerox Alto's mouse driven and graphical user interface.
  • 1985 - Steve Jobs was forced out of Apple, the company he had co-founded, after a long struggle with the company's board and its then-CEO John Sculley. In this same year, Jobs took a few members of Apple to found NeXT, a computer platform development company that specialized in computers for higher-education and business markets.
  • 1986 - Steve funded the computer graphics division of George Luca's Lucasfilm. The new company was Pixar and it produced the first 3D computer animated feature film Toy Story (1995), and went on to become a major animation studio, producing over 20 films since then.
  • 1997 - Apple acquired NeXT, and Jobs became CEO of his former company within a few months. He was largely responsible for helping revive Appl, which had been on the verge of bankruptcy. He worked closely with designer Jony Ive to develop a line of products that had larger cultural ramifications, beginning in 1997 with the Think different advertising campaign and leading the iMac, iTines, iTunes Store, Apple Store, iPod, iPhone, App Store, and the iPad.
  • 2003 - Jobs was diagnosed with a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor.
  • 2011 - Jobs died of respiratory arrest related to the tumor at the age 56 on October 5, 2011.
